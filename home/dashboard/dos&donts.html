Do's and Don'ts During Disasters

Do's

Stay Calm

Keep a clear mind to make informed decisions. Avoid panic and help others stay calm.

Follow Official Guidelines

Listen to local authorities and follow their instructions. Stay updated via news or official apps.

Evacuate Safely

Use designated evacuation routes. Carry your emergency kit and check on neighbors before evacuating.

Communicate

Keep your phone charged and use it sparingly for emergencies. Use texts to reduce network congestion.

Shelter in a Safe Location

Stay indoors in a safe room away from windows or dangerous objects during disasters like hurricanes.

Be Prepared for Aftershocks

Stay ready for aftershocks after an earthquake. Stay away from windows and heavy objects.

Help Others

Assist neighbors or people in need but prioritize your own safety. Collaborate with relief efforts.

Check for Hazards

Inspect for hazards like gas leaks, broken glass, and report them to authorities.

Sanitize

Clean and disinfect wounds and ensure access to clean water to prevent infections.

Don'ts

Don't Ignore Warnings

Ignoring evacuation orders can put you at greater risk. Stay informed and act on warnings.

Don't Use Dangerous Roads

Avoid driving or walking through flooded areas or dangerous paths during disasters.

Don't Panic

Panic can lead to poor decisions. Stay calm and focus on taking the necessary safety measures.

Don't Overuse Your Phone

Minimize phone calls to avoid clogging communication networks, use texts for non-urgent messages.

Don't Enter Unsafe Buildings

Avoid entering structurally unsound buildings after an earthquake or flood until cleared by authorities.

Don't Spread Rumors

Share only verified information from official sources to avoid spreading panic or confusion.

Don't Neglect Your Health

Avoid contaminated food or water. Ensure sanitary conditions for you and your family.

Don't Attempt to Cross Dangerous Areas

Don't cross rivers, bridges, or unstable ground that may be unsafe without guidance.

Don't Light Matches or Flames (Gas Leaks)

If you suspect a gas leak, avoid lighting matches or using electrical appliances.
